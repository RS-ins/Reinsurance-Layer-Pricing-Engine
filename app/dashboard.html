Premium vs Retention / Attachment
Shows how the selected layer price changes as the entry point moves. Higher retentions or attachments usually reduce expected ceded loss and premium because fewer simulated losses reach the layer.
Premium vs Severity Tail σ
Isolates the effect of the severity tail. Larger σ creates heavier individual losses, increasing both expected ceded loss and tail capital requirements.
Premium vs Claim Frequency λ
Shows the effect of claim count intensity. Higher λ means more expected claims per year, increasing the chance that the treaty attaches and the premium rises.
Premium vs Cost of Capital
Shows the loading effect from risk capital. Expected ceded loss stays fixed while the technical premium increases as the required return on tail capital rises.
